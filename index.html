The 500-Dalton Barrier Rule

Your luxury cream isn't weak. It's too big.

Dermatological pharmacokinetics is unforgiving: to passively cross the lipid pathways of the stratum corneum, a molecule must weigh under 500 Daltons. Native collagen in high-end creams weighs around 300,000 Da. It is mathematically locked out — so it sits on the surface, traps moisture, and can make morning puffiness worse.

Rejouva's Pink Collagen is engineered to 100–243 Da. Small enough to pass straight through — and signal your fibroblasts from the inside.

The dual-action mechanism

Repair from within. Drainage from above.

Two systems working at once: a biochemical signal that rebuilds tissue, and a physical structure that clears the fluid and shadows you wake up with.

1

PDRN · the salvage pathway

Purified salmon polynucleotide binds the adenosine A₂ₐ receptor — downregulating MMP-1 and MMP-3 to halt collagen breakdown — while supplying ready-made nucleotide bases so exhausted cells repair without burning ATP on energy-hungry synthesis.

2

Pink Collagen · sub-barrier signal

At 100–243 Da, these peptides cross the stratum corneum and act as biochemical messengers — binding fibroblast receptors to drive a clinically observed 184% increase in endogenous collagen, restoring density to thin periorbital skin.

3

Hydrostatic drainage

The weight of the dense, non-slip hydrogel applies gentle continuous pressure — driving pooled interstitial fluid out of the loose under-eye tissue and back into the lymphatic system. The bags you woke up with, pressed away.

4

Thermal vasoconstriction

As the hydrogel's water evaporates it cools the skin, narrowing the dilated capillaries that show through as blue-purple shadows — visibly clearing vascular dark circles while you get on with your morning.
