Why it isn't really about sleep

The skin under your eye is thinner than a credit card. Almost nothing cushions it. When you're on screens at night, the blue light tells your brain it's still daytime. Your cortisol stays elevated. That keeps the tiny blood vessels around your eyes dilated and slightly leaky. Fluid seeps into the tissue underneath.

When you sleep flat, nothing drains that fluid. It sits there all night. By morning, you have bags. The pooled blood in those capillaries shows through that paper-thin skin as dark purple shadow.

This isn't an aging problem. It's not a sleep problem. It's a fluid and circulation problem — the direct biological cost of working the way you work. A real solution has to drain the accumulated fluid, shrink the dilated vessels, and rebuild the skin structure that chronic cortisol degrades over time. Your eye cream was never doing any of those things.
